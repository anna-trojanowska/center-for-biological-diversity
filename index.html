Tell Obama: Declare Climate Emergency, Ban Oil Exports

The end-of-year budget deal signed into law contains a provision repealing the 40-year-old ban on the export of crude oil, which could increase domestic oil production by more than 3 million barrels a day — an alarming figure for our climate and species like corals and polar bears.

Tell the president to declare a climate emergency and reinstate the ban on crude oil exports.

Keep it in the Ground

Ending new fossil fuel leasing on America's public lands and offshore areas would keep up to 450 billion tons of greenhouse gases from polluting our atmosphere, says a new study commissioned by the Center and Friends of the Earth.

Nuestro Trabajo en México

Mexico hosts a stunning array of species, but too many are imperiled. The Center just opened an office in La Paz to help save sea turtles, vaquitas and other wildlife in trouble. Read about our Mexico work en español or in English.

Study: Public-lands Grazing Cost Taxpayers $1 Billion Over Past Decade

An analysis commissioned by the Center finds that U.S. taxpayers have lost more than $1 billion over the past decade on a program that allows grazing on public lands — and is operating on a staggering loss. In 2014 alone taxpayers lost $125 million in grazing subsidies on federal land.

Bring Back the Bears

The grizzly bear is a main feature of California’s state flag — yet for nearly a century, it's been missing from the Golden State, including in the remote Sierra Nevada. Check out our website and sign our petition to help these endangered bears return to California.
